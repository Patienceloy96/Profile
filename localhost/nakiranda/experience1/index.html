My experience at Clarke international University.

WEEK ONE

Technical session

I was introduced to HTML and CSS and by the end of the week I had created;

  • 3 web pages
  • A sign up form
  • A login Form

Visual design:

This helped me be more creative as I work on my pages and site.

The four principle of visual design are;

  • Contrast
  • Reputation
  • Alignment
  • Proximity

WEEK TWO

Technical session:

I was introduced to word press. Word press had less coding unlike HTML and CSS and made work a little easier.

Working with themes and Plugins makes creation of  web pages and sites so much easier.

I used word press to create my first website.

Leadership Session

This session made me think about what my purpose in life is, and how I can achieve it. I also noted that building relationships with people is really important.

As a young entrepreneur, I noticed that I only not need Financial capital but also Educational, Reputation  and Relational Capital. I actually realized that I mainly need Relational capital rather than Financial capital.

WEEK THREE

Technical session

I completed working on my website.

I was introduced to version control, Version control systems and tools.

I signed up for GitHub. Working with Git and GitHub increased collaboration with course mates, enhanced development of skills on individual basis and as a group.

Leadership Session

I was assigned to read habit 3 of “the seven habits of highly effective people by Stephen R. Covey.” From this I learnt that I need to;

  • Make decisions/choices & act in accordance with them.
  • Organize and execute my priorities.
  • Value activities that are important but not urgent.

WEEK FOUR

Technical session

I worked with GitHub and GitHub desktop.

I created a local repository, forked a central repository, learnt how to create, upload and edit a file, to pull a request and push a file from GitHub desktop to GitHub.  I also learnt how to clone/download a repository.

I hosted my website.

Voice of a Customer

I got to know who a customer is and why it’s important to understand and listen to the customer.

A blue ocean business strategy is best way to run a business where we perform well on what is really important to our customers.

I learnt persuasive ways of selling to customers that start with ‘the hook” ie understanding the situation of your customer before telling them about your product/service.
